# Exercise - Navigate the Portal

In this exercise, you will learn how to navigate the Azure portal. You will look at the following configuration areas:

* Services blade
* Notifications blade
* Cloud Shell
* Settings
* Feedback blade
* Help blade
* Directory and Subscription blade

## Services Blade

1. In the left-hand pane, click **All services**. Take a couple of minutes to scroll down the list to see how many services Azure offers.

2. Click **Virtual machines**. The **Virtual Machines** blade appears. There is nothing currently showing because you have not defined any virtual machines.

3. Click the **Create virtual machine** button. The **Compute** blade appears, as in the previous exercise.

4. Click the X in the top right-hand corner to close the **Compute** blade.

5. Click the X in the top right-hand corner to close the **Virtual machines** blade. You should now be back at the Dashboard page.

## Notifications Blade

1. On the top menu on the right side, click the bell icon. You can now see any notifications.

2. If any notifications appear, on one of them, hover your mouse over, then click the X to dismiss that notification.

3. To the right of **Dismiss**, click **All**. You should have no notifications showing.

4. Click the X in the top right-hand corner to close the **Notifications** pane.

## Cloud Shell

1. Click the Cloud Shell icon (>_) to create a cloud shell session.

2. Click **Bash (Linux)** or **PowerShell (Linux)**, depending on your personal preferences.

3. In the **You have no storage mounted** screen, click **Create storage**. The Cloud Shell window appears at the bottom of the screen.

4. In the prompt, type *AZ Help* and press Enter.

5. Click the language dropdown on the left of the bar and change the scripting language from PowerShell to Bash from Bash to PowerShell, depending on which option you selecteds. Click **Restart** when prompted. The screen displays **Restarting your Cloud Shell**.

6. In the restarted shell, type *AZ Help* and press Enter.

7. In the Cloud Shell window, click the Settings (cog) icon. Note that you can change the text size and the font.

8. Click the X in the top right-hand corner to close the Cloud Shell pane.

## Settings

1. Click the cog icon to open the **Portal settings** pane.

2. Drop down the **Log me out when inactive** setting and select **After one hour**.

3. Under **Choose a theme**, select the different themes and choose the one you like the best.

4. Under **High contrast theme**, try the three different options.

5. Disable Toast notifications.

6. Note the double-click to change the dashboard theme setting.

7. Click **Apply** to accept your changes.

8. Under **Language**, select **Espagnol**, then click the **Refresh** button. If a **Translate this page** dialog box appears, close the box. Note that the whole portal is now in Spanish.

9. Click the cog icon in the top menu bar and under **Language**, select **English**. Click the **Actualizar** button. The portal returns to English.

## Feedback Blade

1. Click the smiley face icon to open the **Send us feedback** blade.

2. Type your impressions of Azure into the **Tell us about your experience** box, click the box that says **Microsoft can email you about your feedback**, and click **Submit Feedback**.

3. A **Feedback sent** message will appear, then close. You should now be back at the Dashboard.

## Help Blade

1. Click the question mark to show the **Help** blade.

2. Click the **Help and Support** button.

3. In the **Help + Support** blade, under **Support**, click **New support request**.

4. In the Basics blade, under **Issue type**, select **Technical**.

5. Under **Subscription**, click your subscription name.

6. Under **Service**, select **Virtual Machine running Windows**.

7. Because you do not have a paid support plan, there are limited support options. Under **Upgrade your support plan**, click **Choose a plan**.

8. In the **Support Plans** blade, there are four available plans - Basic, Developer, Standard, and Professional Direct. Compare the options offered by each plan, then click the X at the top right to close the **Support Plans blade**.

9. Close the **Support options** blade.

10. Close the **Basics** blade.

11. Close the **New support request** blade.

12. Close the **Help and Support** blade.

13. Click the Help icon again.

14. Click **What's new** to see what features have recently been released. Also note the other help options:

* Azure roadmap
* Launch guided tour
* Keyboard shortcuts
* Show diagnostics
* Privacy + terms

15. Click the X in the top right-hand corner to close the Help blade.

16. Close the **What's new** blade. You should now be back to the Dashboard.

## Directory and Subscription

1. Click the Book and Filter icon to show the **Directory + subscription** blade. Because you do not have additional directories, you will not be able to switch directories.

2. Click the X in the top right-hand corner to close the **Directory + subscription** blade.

## Profile Settings

1. Click on your name in the top right-hand corner of the Portal. Options include:

    * Sign out of Azure
    * Change password
    * Change contact information
    * View permissions
    * Submit an idea to the Azure team
    * View your bill
    * Switch directory (shows the Directory + Subscription blade as in the previous section)

2. Click **View my bill** takes you to the **Cost Management + Billing - Invoices** page, which helps you analyze where Azure is generating costs.

3. Under **Subscription or reserved instance**, select your subscription.

4. Click a billing period.

5. Note the service costs - these should be minimal with your current subscription.

6. Click the X in the top right-hand corner to close the **Costs by service** blade.

7. Click the X in the top right-hand corner to close the **Cost Management + Billing - Invoices** page.

8. You should now be back at the Dashboard.

## Summary

In this exercise, you have reviewed the basic settings and configuration options in the Azure portal. In the next unit, you will learn how to configure dashboards.
