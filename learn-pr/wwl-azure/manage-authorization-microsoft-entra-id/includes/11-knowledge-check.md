## Multiple Choice
What is the purpose of Privileged Identity Management?
( ) Allows users to manage their own access permissions {{Incorrect. This would increase the risk of unnecessary access permissions.}}
( ) Provides unlimited access permissions to all resources {{Incorrect. This would increase the risk of excessive and misused access permissions.}}
(x) Enables the automation and validation of the creation and teardown of environments to help deliver secure and stable application hosting platforms {{Correct. Privileged Identity Management provides time-based and approval-based role activation to mitigate the risks of excessive, unnecessary, or misused access permissions on resources that you care about.}}

## Multiple Choice
What is the purpose of Just In Time and Just Enough Access policies when assigning roles?
( ) To grant unlimited access to all users {{Incorrect. Granting unlimited access to all users is not aligned with Zero Trust guiding principles.}}
( ) To assign roles permanently without any time restrictions {{Incorrect. Assigning roles permanently without any time restrictions is not aligned with Just In Time and Just Enough Access policies.}}
(x) Enables the automation and validation of the creation and teardown of environments to help deliver secure and stable application hosting platforms {{Correct. Just In Time and Just Enough Access policies align with Zero Trust guiding principles by granting access only when needed and for a limited time period, reducing the attack surface and minimizing the risk of unauthorized access.}}
